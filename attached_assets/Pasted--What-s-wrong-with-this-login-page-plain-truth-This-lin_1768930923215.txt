🚨 What’s wrong with this login page (plain truth)

This line at the bottom:

“Don’t have an account? Start your free trial”

is now out of alignment with:

Your new signup framing

Your activation flow

Your pricing psychology

The behavior you’re trying to change

Even worse, it’s appearing at a high-intent moment (login), where users are already oriented toward action.

Why this matters more than it seems

When someone sees “free trial” here, it:

Reintroduces trial thinking

Signals “temporary” again

Conflicts with “Activate LeaseShield”

Trains users to delay commitment

Creates inconsistency across the product

Consistency matters a lot for trust and conversion.

Right now, your product says:

Signup page: Be ready for your next applicant

Activation page: Activate LeaseShield

Login page: Start your free trial

That’s three different mental models. We want one.

✅ What this page should communicate instead

This page should reinforce:

“LeaseShield is a tool you set up and keep ready.”

Not:

“LeaseShield is something you try.”

✅ EXACT changes to make (copy-paste ready)
🔁 1. Replace the bottom CTA text

Current:

Don’t have an account? Start your free trial

Replace with (best option):

Don’t have an account? Create an account

Alternate acceptable options:

Get started

Set up your account

But Create an account is the cleanest.

🔁 2. (Optional but recommended) Tighten the subheadline

Current:

Sign in to access your landlord protection tools

This is fine, but slightly generic.

Recommended replacement:

Sign in to manage applications, screening, and decisions in one place

This reinforces why they’re here.

🔁 3. (Optional) Add a subtle reassurance line at the bottom

Below the button or near the footer, small gray text:

$10/month • Cancel anytime

This quietly reinforces the low-risk nature without selling.

🚫 What NOT to do on this page

Do not:

Mention trials

Mention annual pricing

Add marketing copy

Add testimonials

Add feature lists

This page’s job is access, not persuasion.
