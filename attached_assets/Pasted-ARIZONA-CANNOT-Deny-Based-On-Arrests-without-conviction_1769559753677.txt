ARIZONA

CANNOT Deny Based On:
• Arrests without convictions
• Sealed or expunged criminal records
• Lawful source of income including Housing Choice Vouchers (statewide protection effective 2023)
• Protected classes: sexual orientation, gender identity, veteran status

CAN Deny Based On:
• Convictions that pose a demonstrable risk to property or safety
• Verified eviction judgments
• Poor credit history tied to housing obligations
• Inability to meet income or rental criteria

Must Follow These Rules:
• Must provide adverse action notice if using a consumer report
• Must apply screening criteria consistently
• Criminal history must be related to legitimate business necessity

Key Statute: Arizona Fair Housing Act, A.R.S. § 41-1491

CALIFORNIA

CANNOT Deny Based On:
• Arrests not resulting in conviction
• Convictions older than 7 years (most cases)
• Criminal records that are sealed, dismissed, or expunged
• Housing vouchers or rental assistance
• Eviction filings without judgments
• Protected classes include source of income, sexual orientation, gender identity

CAN Deny Based On:
• Recent convictions with a direct safety risk
• Eviction judgments within lawful lookback
• Verified nonpayment history
• Poor credit related to housing debt

Must Follow These Rules:
• Must assess criminal history after application (ban-the-box)
• Must perform individualized assessment before denial
• Adverse action notice required
• Credit reporting limited to 7-year lookback

Key Statute: California Fair Employment and Housing Act (Gov. Code § 12955)
