CITY-LEVEL TENANT SCREENING RULES

(These rules APPLY IN ADDITION to state law)
If your property is in one of these cities, follow these extra rules.

ARIZONA
PHOENIX

You CANNOT deny based on:
• Arrests that did not lead to a conviction
• Very old criminal convictions that have nothing to do with housing safety

You CAN deny based on:
• Recent convictions that clearly relate to safety or property damage
• Eviction judgments (not just filings)

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• Criminal history must relate to resident safety
• Do not automatically deny “any criminal record”

CALIFORNIA
LOS ANGELES

You CANNOT deny based on:
• Any arrest without a conviction
• Criminal convictions older than 7 years
• Eviction filings where the tenant was not evicted
• Use of housing vouchers or rental assistance

You CAN deny based on:
• Recent, serious convictions that pose a real safety risk
• Eviction judgments within legal limits

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• You must review criminal history after the application
• You must consider the time passed and the type of offense
• You must give a written reason if you deny based on criminal history

SAN FRANCISCO

You CANNOT deny based on:
• Any arrest that did not result in conviction
• Any conviction older than 7 years
• Non-violent convictions unrelated to housing
• Eviction filings without a judgment

You CAN deny based on:
• Recent, serious convictions tied to safety

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• Criminal screening happens later in the process
• You must give the applicant a chance to explain
• Written notice is required before and after denial

OAKLAND

You CANNOT deny based on:
• Criminal records older than 7 years
• Sealed, dismissed, or expunged records
• Eviction filings

You CAN deny based on:
• Recent convictions tied to safety

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• You must allow the applicant to provide context
• Denials must be narrowly based on real risk

ILLINOIS
CHICAGO

You CANNOT deny based on:
• Arrests without conviction
• Convictions older than 3 years (with very limited exceptions)
• Sealed or expunged records
• Eviction filings without a judgment
• Housing vouchers or lawful income

You CAN deny based on:
• Recent convictions that affect safety
• Eviction judgments

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• Criminal review happens after conditional approval
• You must give the applicant a chance to respond
• Written explanation required if denying

MICHIGAN
DETROIT

You CANNOT deny based on:
• Arrests without conviction
• Sealed or expunged records

You CAN deny based on:
• Convictions that clearly relate to safety or property risk

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• Criminal history must directly relate to housing risk
• Adverse action notice required

NEVADA
LAS VEGAS / CLARK COUNTY

You CANNOT deny based on:
• Housing vouchers or rental assistance
• Arrests without conviction

You CAN deny based on:
• Relevant criminal convictions
• Eviction judgments

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• Screening rules must be applied the same to everyone

NEW MEXICO
ALBUQUERQUE

You CANNOT deny based on:
• Arrests without conviction
• Sealed or expunged records
• Housing vouchers
• Eviction filings without judgment

You CAN deny based on:
• Convictions tied to safety
• Eviction judgments

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• Criminal history must show real housing risk
• Individual review is required

NORTH CAROLINA
CHARLOTTE

You CANNOT deny based on:
• Arrests without conviction

You CAN deny based on:
• Convictions related to safety
• Eviction judgments

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• Screening policies must be consistent

OHIO
COLUMBUS

You CANNOT deny based on:

You CAN deny based on:
• Relevant convictions
• Eviction judgments

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
• Follow fair housing rules strictly
• Adverse action notice required

You CANNOT deny based on:

You CAN deny based on:
• Eviction judgments

You CAN deny based on:

Extra Rules You Must Follow:
