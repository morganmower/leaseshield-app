Critical Risk Corrections
A. California – “3 Court Days” Is Incorrect

California 3-Day Notice to Pay Rent or Quit under CCP §1161(2):

It is 3 days

Excludes judicial holidays

Saturdays count

Sundays count

Court holidays do not

Do not label it “court days.” That phrase creates confusion and potential miscalculation.

You said you are not auto-calculating dates — that reduces liability, but:

You must include:

“The deadline excludes judicial holidays.”

Otherwise you are silently exposing users.

B. Florida – 3 Business Days

Florida requires:

3 business days

Excludes weekends AND legal holidays

If you are not calculating dates, the form must clearly state:

“The three-day period excludes Saturdays, Sundays, and legal holidays.”

Otherwise landlords will miscount.

C. Nevada – 5 Judicial Days

Nevada requires judicial days (court days).

That is not the same as business days.

You cannot safely avoid explaining this.

D. Utah – Calendar Days

Utah 3 calendar days includes weekends and holidays.

Utah landlords often miscalculate. Your system should explicitly label it:

“3 calendar days (includes weekends and holidays).”
