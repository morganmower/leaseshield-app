Disable them completely. Do not “update” them.

Long answer (and this matters):

Why trial-expiration emails must die entirely

Trial expiration emails are built on the wrong psychological premise for your product.

They assume:

A daily-use habit

A ticking clock

Missed opportunity urgency

LeaseShield is the opposite:

Event-triggered

Calm, readiness-based

Used when a decision arises

If you “update the copy” but keep the trigger, you still send the wrong signal:

“You missed your chance.”

That message:

Creates guilt

Triggers avoidance

Lowers future conversion

Makes people less likely to return when they actually need you

Even a perfectly written expiration email would still be strategically wrong.

What to do instead (clean break)
✅ Step 1: Disable all trial expiration automations

No “trial ending soon”

No “trial expired”

No countdown language anywhere

No reminders tied to time passing

This is not a copy problem. It’s a trigger problem.

✅ Step 2: Replace with the 3-email lifecycle you already approved

You already have the correct system:

Immediate framing email (ready when it matters)

3-day calm nudge (activation reminder)

10–14 day close-the-loop email (then silence)

That’s it.

Why this will increase conversions (counterintuitive but true)

Landlords do not respond well to:

SaaS urgency

Artificial deadlines

“You’re missing out” messaging

They respond to:

Trust

Relevance

Memory jogs tied to real events

When the next applicant shows up, they’ll think:

“Oh yeah — LeaseShield.”

Not:

“Ugh, another SaaS email.”

One nuance (important)

You can still send an email if an event happens.

Examples of good triggers:

They open the Screening Decoder but don’t run it

They start an application but don’t finish

They paste data but hit an activation wall

Those are behavior-based nudges, not time-based pressure.

Final instruction (clear and firm)

✔️ Disable trial expiration emails entirely
❌ Do not reword or soften them
✔️ Replace with calm, event-anchored lifecycle emails
✔️ Let silence work in your favor

This is a maturity move. Most founders never make it because it feels uncomfortable. But it’s exactly right for your product.
