First: the principle (this matters)

Do not “remind” people in a way that feels like chasing.
Do re-anchor them to the moment of value.

LeaseShield is:

Event-triggered

Trust-based

Low urgency until suddenly very urgent

So your emails should feel like:

“This is here when it matters.”

Not:

“Hey don’t forget us.”

The correct lifecycle (going forward)

You only need 3 automated emails total + 1 manual play.

That’s it.

🧩 PART 1: New signups going forward (automation)
Email #1 — Immediately after signup

Purpose: Frame the product correctly (not features)

Subject: LeaseShield is ready when you need it

Body:

Hi {{FirstName}},

Your LeaseShield account is set up.

You don’t need to use it every day — it’s there for the moments that matter:
• When a screening report raises questions
• When you want to stay consistent and defensible
• When you’d rather not guess

If you have an applicant coming up, start with the Screening Decoder.
That’s where most landlords see the value immediately.

— Morgan


No CTA button. No pricing. Calm.

Email #2 — 3 days later (only if NOT activated)

Purpose: Nudge toward activation without pressure

Subject: When screening gets tricky

Body:

Hi {{FirstName}},

Quick note.

Most landlords don’t think about screening tools until a report raises questions.
That’s exactly when LeaseShield earns its keep.

If you want it ready before the next applicant shows up, you can activate anytime from your account.

No rush either way — just wanted to make sure you saw where the value usually clicks.

— Morgan


That’s it.

Email #3 — 10–14 days later (final touch)

Purpose: Close the loop respectfully

Subject: Closing the loop

Body:

Hi {{FirstName}},

I’ll leave you alone after this.

LeaseShield is built for landlords who already run real screenings and want clarity when decisions matter.
If that’s useful down the road, your account will be waiting.

Either way, appreciate you checking it out.

— Morgan


After this: silence.
No drip. No nurture. No marketing spam.

This restraint actually increases long-term conversion.

🧩 PART 2: What to do with former trial users (right now)

This is a one-time cleanup email, not ongoing.

Who gets it:

People who trialed

Liked the product

Did not activate

Are currently inactive

Send one email only.

Subject: When the next applicant comes up

Body (very important — send exactly this tone):

Hi {{FirstName}},

You tried LeaseShield earlier, so I wanted to close the loop properly.

Most landlords don’t need tools like this every month — they need them when a screening report raises questions.
That’s why we rebuilt the flow so LeaseShield can simply stay ready in the background.

If you have an applicant coming up and want clarity without guessing, you can activate anytime from your account.

If not, no worries at all — just wanted to make sure you knew it’s there.

— Morgan


No links.
No pricing.
No CTA button.

This email works because it:

Respects autonomy

Reframes usage

Removes guilt

Keeps the door open

🧠 PART 3: What NOT to do (important)

Do not:

Send weekly reminders

Send “your trial expired” emails

Offer discounts

Add urgency

Ask “any questions?” repeatedly

Those all signal insecurity and reduce trust.

🧩 PART 4: The long-term compounding play (quiet but powerful)

Add one in-app reminder only, not email:

For inactive users, a subtle banner:

LeaseShield is inactive — activate when you want it ready for your next applicant.

That’s it.

The moment they have an applicant, they’ll remember.

Final operating rule (write this down)

We only email when we’re re-anchoring to a real screening moment — never to “check in.”

If you follow that rule, LeaseShield will convert slower but cleaner, and churn will stay low.
