Fixing the Squished Spacing After the Header
The current layout packs too much vertically in a small area, which can feel overwhelming or hard to scan (especially on desktop or tablet).
Quick Wins:

Increase vertical padding/margin between elements (aim for 16–24px between header/description, privacy box, and quick buttons).
Add more breathing room around the quick buttons row (e.g., 20–32px margin below the privacy box).
Make the header area taller if needed (add subtle background color or a thin divider line below the privacy box to separate sections visually).

Why this helps: It reduces visual density, making the interface feel calmer and easier to read—key for stressed users.
2. Making Quick-Start Buttons More Inviting & Clickable
The current flat text buttons don't signal "tap me" well—they look like regular body text. Best practices for clickable elements (from 2025–2026 UX trends) emphasize:

Visual cues like shadows, borders, rounded corners, hover effects, and contrast to make them feel raised/3D.
Consistent padding and size for easy tapping (min 44x44px on mobile).
Hover/active states (e.g., color change, slight lift) to confirm interactivity.

Recommended Changes (Tailwind-friendly examples):

Turn them into small pill-shaped or rounded cards with subtle shadow/border.
Use your site's accent color (teal/green from screenshots) for background on hover.
Add hover effect: background lightens, cursor pointer, slight scale-up.
