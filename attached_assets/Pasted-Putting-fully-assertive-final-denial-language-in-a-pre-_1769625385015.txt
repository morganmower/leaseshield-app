Putting fully assertive, final denial language in a pre-adverse notice is risky, because pre-adverse must remain tentative.

But removing reasons entirely also causes problems:

landlords forget why they denied

inconsistent explanations later

weak audit trail

So the answer is two parallel records:

Internal decision record (always detailed)

Applicant-facing letter (carefully phrased)

The correct model (simple + defensible)
1️⃣ INTERNAL: always store the full reason (no compromise)

In LeaseShield, you should always store:

The exact denial criteria selected

The exact “safe denial sentence”

Jurisdiction + ruleset version

Timestamp

This is your audit trail and landlord memory.

This internal record can safely say:

“Based on our written screening criteria, the applicant’s felony conviction presents a documented risk to resident safety or property.”

That language is fine internally, because it’s not being sent yet.

2️⃣ PRE-ADVERSE: include the reason, but soften the language

You can include the reason in a pre-adverse notice — it just cannot sound final.

✅ Correct way to do it

Use tentative phrasing and reference the report, not the applicant.

Example (production-safe):

Reason(s) Under Consideration:
Based on our written screening criteria, certain information contained in the consumer report may not meet the qualification requirements for this property.

If you want to be more specific (only when legal):

Based on our written screening criteria, a recent criminal conviction reflected in the consumer report may present a risk related to resident safety or property.

Key words doing the work:

may

reflected in the consumer report

under consideration

This preserves transparency without locking the decision.

3️⃣ POST-ADVERSE: be specific and final

This is where the exact reason belongs, clearly and plainly.

Example (post-adverse):

The application was denied because it did not meet our written screening criteria. Specifically, a recent criminal conviction identified in the consumer report presents a documented risk to resident safety or property.

This is:

clear

honest

compliant

defensible
