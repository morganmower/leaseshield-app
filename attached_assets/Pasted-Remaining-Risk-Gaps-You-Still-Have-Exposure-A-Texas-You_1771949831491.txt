Remaining Risk Gaps (You Still Have Exposure)
A. Texas — You Oversimplified

Texas

You correctly note:

“3 days (default, unless lease specifies otherwise)”

But your system currently does not force the user to confirm the lease terms.

Risk:

If the lease says 5 days and you generate a 3-day notice, the eviction can be dismissed.

Required addition:

Before generating Texas notice:

“Does your lease specify a notice period for nonpayment?”
☐ Yes — enter number of days
☐ No — use statutory default (3 days)

If “Yes,” require entry. Lock notice period to that value.

Without that, Texas is a silent defect state.

B. Nevada — Judicial Day Counting Is Non-Trivial

Nevada

You’re handling judicial days correctly conceptually.

But:

Nevada district courts observe specific holiday calendars.

Judicial holidays are not always identical to federal holidays.

If your calculator is wrong once, you lose credibility.

Mitigation:

Use Nevada Judicial Branch published holiday schedule annually.

Version your holiday tables by year.

Log holiday table source date in admin.

This cannot be static logic.

C. Florida — Payment Location Is Not Optional

Florida

You correctly flagged:

Must include where rent can be paid.

You must also:

Require hours of availability if payment location is in-person.

If payment is by mail, ensure mailing address is included.

If electronic payment allowed, verify lease permits it.

Otherwise the notice can be challenged as defective.

Add conditional validation:

If payment method = in person → require address + hours.
If payment method = mail → require mailing address.
If payment method = electronic → require lease authorization confirmation.

D. California — Bigger Risk Than You’re Accounting For

California

Your disclaimer is good.

But the real landmine is this:

AB 1482 “just cause” and relocation requirements are not merely local — they’re statewide.

Your 30-day / 60-day notices are insufficient in:

Covered properties

No-fault termination scenarios

If you generate a 30-day notice without checking:

Is property exempt from AB 1482?

Has tenant occupied > 12 months?

Is relocation assistance required?

You may generate a legally defective notice.

Mitigation:

Before generating CA termination notice:

Add gating questions:

Is property subject to AB 1482?

How long has tenant occupied property?

Is termination for at-fault or no-fault reason?

If you do not gate, you are not compliance-grade in California.
