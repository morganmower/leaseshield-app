3️⃣ Texas — Subtle Risk
Texas

Texas posting inside the main entry is allowed if:

Personal delivery is not possible.

That is conditional.

You may want to treat Texas posting similar to CA/NV:
Require confirmation that personal delivery was attempted.

Texas eviction dismissals often happen due to defective service.

4️⃣ Illinois — Be Careful With Rent-Only Assumption
Illinois

You wisely marked:

Rent-only validation pending statute verification.

Do not assume rent-only until statute + case law confirmed.

Illinois sometimes permits certain lease-defined charges if defined as “additional rent.”

If you apply California-style strict rent-only without legal basis, you may incorrectly block valid notices.

Action item:
Confirm whether Illinois allows:

“Rent and other sums due” language

Or strictly rent only

Do not over-restrict unless statute requires it.

5️⃣ Florida — One Edge Case
Florida

Your business-day engine excludes statutory holidays.

Good.

But Florida statute excludes:

Saturdays

Sundays

Legal holidays

If the notice is served by mail, some practitioners add additional mailing time.

You are not calculating service completion timing.

That’s fine because you’re asking for “date notice is served,” not “date mailed.”

Make sure your UI clarifies:

“Enter the date the notice was actually served (not the date you mailed it).”

Otherwise landlords may enter mail date instead of effective service date.
