Where I’m pushing back (important nuance)
⚠️ “BEST VALUE” + “Save $20/year” is the wrong psychology here

That language works great for:

Daily tools

Habit software

Obvious recurring value

It works less well for:

Insurance-like tools

Occasional-use products

Risk-averse landlords

Here’s why:

When a landlord sees:

BEST VALUE

Their brain goes:

“Wait… if I only use this a few times per year, why is annual the ‘best’ option?”

That introduces internal conflict right before checkout.

You don’t want them re-evaluating usage frequency at that moment.

Keep the toggle. Change the framing.
Current (what you described)

Monthly – $10/month (default)

Annual – $100/year

BEST VALUE

Recommended replacement

Monthly (default)
$10/month

Annual (optional)
$100/year

Includes 2 months free
