Why this pricing block should NOT live prominently on the homepage

Your homepage’s primary job is not to sell a plan.
It is to answer one question:

“Can this help me right now with a screening decision?”

Right now, this section pulls the visitor into:

Plan comparison

Savings math

“Best value” evaluation

Commitment thinking

That’s too early for your product.

You already learned this lesson with trials.
This is the same issue in a different costume.

The core problem with this section (objectively)
1️⃣ It reintroduces decision friction

Landlords seeing this think:

“Monthly vs annual?”

“How often will I use this?”

“Is this worth committing to?”

That thinking delays action, which is deadly for an event-triggered product.

2️⃣ “Best Value” + “Save $20” conflicts with your positioning

Your positioning is:

Use it only when it matters.

But this block says:

Optimize your subscription choice.

Those two messages fight each other.

You just removed this language from the activation flow for good reason — it shouldn’t live loudly on the homepage either.

3️⃣ Homepage ≠ Checkout

This pricing layout is checkout-grade UI:

Feature checklists

Savings math

Value badges

Dual CTAs

That belongs after intent is established, not before.

What you SHOULD do instead (best practice for your product)
✅ Keep pricing accessible, but not dominant

Here’s the correct structure:

Recommended homepage structure (high-level)
1️⃣ Hero (primary)

Pain-first headline

“Explain My Screening Report” CTA

No pricing math

No plan comparison

2️⃣ How it helps (secondary)

Decoder

Applications → screening

Compliance protection

No cards. No badges. No savings math.
