What I’d change in your copy (so it’s clearer + less “tool-y”)

Use: “Go to Screening Toolkit → Denial Decision Assistant”

Make Step 2 even more idiot-proof

“Check what appeared in the report. The app will tell you what you can and cannot use as a denial reason in your location.”

Rename the status labels (small change, big clarity)

BLOCKED → Not Allowed

ALLOWED → Allowed to Consider

CONDITIONAL → Allowed, but Extra Steps Required

That one phrase (“Allowed to Consider”) avoids the lawsuit-y implication that “Allowed = you’re safe to deny.”

“If a consumer report was used, we’ll also generate the required adverse action notice.”
This reinforces the value they’re paying for.

Quick walkthrough example (Utah vs California)
Example A: Salt Lake City, Utah

Arrest only → Not Allowed

Conviction within 7 years → Allowed to Consider

Eviction filing only → Warning / often not strong enough alone (push them toward “judgment” proof)

Voucher → Not Allowed in Salt Lake City

Decision: Deny → generates 1-sentence denial + adverse action if report used

Example B: Los Angeles, California

Arrest only → Not Allowed

Conviction older than 7 years → Not Allowed

Conviction within 7 years → Extra Steps Required (individualized assessment flow)

Voucher → Not Allowed

Eviction filing only → Not Allowed

Decision: Deny → only possible if they completed the required steps and selected a lawful reason

One more pushback (important)

Make sure your UI never implies: “ALLOWED = you’re safe to deny.”
Your phrasing should always be: “Allowed to consider under local rules.” That alone reduces bad landlord behavior.
