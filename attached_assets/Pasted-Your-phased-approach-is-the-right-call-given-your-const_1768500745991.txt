The key: make the AI “dynamic” using only the user’s question

Since you’ll never have the report, the AI needs to:

infer what risk dimension the question implies (recency, count, severity, pattern)

respond with:

plain-English meaning

how landlords weigh it

“what it does NOT mean” that is risk-calibrated

“where to look” without asking them to paste report content

optional follow-ups that are yes/no or non-sensitive

This is prompt-level, not architecture-level.

Phase 1: Replit-ready implementation (simple changes)
1) Add a “Caution Level” derived from keywords + topic

You already have topicClassifier.ts. Add one light helper:

Inputs: topic, questionText
Outputs: cautionLevel = low | medium | high + triggers[]

Example triggers:

Collections: “3”, “multiple”, “recent”, “2024”, “2025”, “placed”, “open”, “unpaid”

Criminal: “felony”, “violent”, “recent”, “weapon”, “domestic”

Eviction: “UD”, “judgment”, “writ”, “recent”

This is NOT “scoring their report.” It’s just calibrating tone based on what they asked.

2) Build a “Field Reference Guide” per topic (static map)

This is the magic that makes answers feel practical even with no data.

Example (Collections topic):

Where to look:

Placed/Clsd (recency)

Status (open vs paid/settled)

Balance/Amount

Creditor type (medical vs utility vs credit card, if shown)

Do the same for bankruptcies, charge-offs, lates, utilization, evictions, etc.

3) Replace “What This Does NOT Mean” with a tone-gated template

Rule: It must always contain:

1 line that prevents overreach (“doesn’t automatically mean X”)

1 line that prevents false reassurance (“but if [trigger], treat as meaningful risk signal”)

1 line about consistency/fair-housing (“apply written criteria consistently”)

Tone gates:

Low: more neutral

Medium: “real risk flag—don’t ignore”

High: “higher-risk indicator—slow down, verify, be consistent”

4) Add thumbs 👍 👎 + store to DB

Store:

topic

cautionLevel

questionText

responseText

rating

timestamp

(optional) “internal notes” or “admin edited version”

That gives you data for Phase 2.

Phase 2: Curated example library (simple and powerful)

I agree with your idea: don’t do full RAG yet.

Do this instead:

create a small examples/ library keyed by {topic, cautionLevel}

include 2–4 “best responses” per bucket

inject 1 example into the prompt based on the current bucket

This gives you “learning” without latency or complexity.

Phase 3: Only if you hit limits

You only need full rubric / retrieval when:

you start getting inconsistent outputs across similar questions

you need stricter tone control for compliance reasons

you want multi-turn “interview mode” that adapts deeply

Until then, prompt + keyword caution + field guide + feedback is the move.
