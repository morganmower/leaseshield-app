⚠️ Issue #1: Too many checkboxes in “Required Notices Checklist”

From a legal standpoint, you’re right.
From a new landlord brain, this feels like:

“Am I supposed to know which of these apply?”

They might ask:

“Do I check Pre-Adverse or not?”

“What happens if I miss one?”

“Why am I checking things the system already knows?”

You already know the answer programmatically.

⚠️ Issue #2: “Save & Log Decision” vs “Download Letter”

Right now there are two strong CTAs:

Save & Log Decision

Preview & Download Letter

A new landlord may wonder:

“Do I need to do both?”

“Which one matters legally?”

“What if I just download the letter?”

You and I know they should do both. The UI doesn’t quite say that yet.

⚠️ Issue #3: The audit trail value is invisible

You are doing something extremely powerful behind the scenes — but the landlord doesn’t fully feel it yet.

“Decision Logged” is good, but they don’t yet internalize:

“Oh — this protects me later.”

That’s a messaging opportunity.

3️⃣ Changes I Would Make (All Small, High-Impact)
🔧 Change #1: Convert the checklist into “System-Managed Notices”

Instead of checkboxes, I’d do this:

Required Notices (Automatically Included)
✅ Adverse Action Notice
✅ CRA contact information
✅ Right to dispute information

Then conditionally show:

Pre-Adverse Action Notice
Required in your location before final denial
[Generate Pre-Adverse Notice]

This removes decision anxiety and reinforces that LeaseShield is handling it.

🔧 Change #2: Merge “Save & Log” into the letter flow

I’d strongly recommend:

Clicking Preview & Download Adverse Action Letter automatically:

saves the decision

locks the denial text

logs the audit trail

Then replace “Save & Log Decision” with:

Decision saved automatically for your records

This removes ambiguity and ensures compliance happens even if they rush.

🔧 Change #3: Add one sentence that explicitly sells the audit trail

Right after the green confirmation banner, add:

What this means:
This record shows which rules applied, what information you relied on, and the exact notice provided — in case the decision is ever questioned.

This single sentence makes the subscription feel worth it.

🔧 Change #4: Minor wording tweaks for extra clarity

Very small changes, big clarity gains:

“Allowed to Consider” → “Allowed to Consider (if consistent with your criteria)”

“Extra Steps Required” → “Extra Steps Required (we’ll guide you)”

These reduce the risk of overconfidence.
